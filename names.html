That't not my name, that's not mah name!

Date created: March 2021
Tools used: R (ggplot2, gganimate)

My name is Adrienne and it's pronounced A-dree-anne or A-dree-un. It is an uncommon name in Singapore and often confused with Adrian, the male version of this name. Often times when I tell people my name, I will get a confused reaction: "Adri- What?" or "Isn't that a guy's name?"

In this visualisation, I wanted to look at the history of the name Adrian (and its different variants, e.g. Adrienne, Adrien, Adrianne, Adriane...) over time to see whether it has always been a male name. To my surprise, that hasn't always been the case.

Number of boys and girls named Adrian (and it's variants) over time


After taking a closer look, it's clear that the number of boys and girls called Adrian were roughly the same up until around 1980s. At some point in the 1980s, there was a departure from the trend. The number of boys named Adrian continued to rise but there was a sharp decrease in girls named Adrian.

So what happened before the 1980s?
Why was the name so popular among girls? Turns out, the name owes its popularity to the female celebrities in the 20th century. During that time, contemporary actress Adrienne Barbeau starred in a highly popular sitcom (1972-1978). The number of girls named Adrian peaked in the late 70s and early 80s. The famous actress had made the name very popular among girls as boys.

In fact, between the 30s and the 70s, you're more likely to find a girl called Adrian than a boy. The name's popularty shot up by 49% in one year alone (1932) following the release of an American movie called "The Death Kiss", starring actress Adrienne Ames.

In the 21st century, you're 13 times more likely to find a guy called Adrian than a girl. It's a pity that the name lost it's popularity among girls. Having said that, I adore the name that my parents gave me and enjoyed this fun exercise learning a bit about the history of my name.

Data extracted from the R package called "babynames". Information on my name is from www.ohbabynames.com (Check out your name on their website, you might learn something new :).

# You will need these packages
library(tidyverse)
install.packages("gganimate")
library(gganimate)
install.packages("remotes")
remotes::install_github("R-CoderDotCom/ggcats@main")
library(ggcats)
install.packages('gifski')
install.packages('png')
 # Load and tidy up dataset
library(babynames)
baby_data <- data.frame(babynames)
 baby_data$name <- ifelse(baby_data$name == "Adrien", "Adrienne", ifelse(baby_data$name == "Adrian", "Adrienne", ifelse(baby_data$name == "Adrianne", "Adrienne", baby_data$name))) baby <- baby_data %>%
filter(name == "Adrienne") %>%
dplyr::select(-c(prop)) %>%
dplyr::group_by(year, sex) %>%
summarise(n = sum(n))
 # Choose cat image
baby$cat <- rep(NA, 269)
baby$cat[which(baby$sex == "F")] <- "mouth"
baby$cat[which(baby$sex == "M")] <- "toast"
# Plot gif
ggplot(baby, aes(x = year, y = n, group = sex, color = sex)) +
geom_line(size =2) +
geom_cat(aes(cat = cat), size = 5) +
transition_reveal(year) +
xlab("Year (1880 - 2017)") +
ylab("Count") +
theme_grey() +
theme(legend.position = "none",
plot.title = element_text(size = 20))+
theme(
panel.background = element_rect(fill = "#FFF6EB",
colour = "#FFF6EB",
size = 0.5, linetype = "solid"),
panel.grid.major = element_line(size = 0.5, linetype = 'solid', colour = "white"), panel.grid.minor = element_line(size = 0.25, linetype = 'solid', colour = "white"), plot.background = element_rect(fill = "#FFF6EB"))
