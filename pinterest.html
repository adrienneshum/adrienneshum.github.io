Overview

Pinterest is a popular visual discover engine but has struggled to increase user engagement amongst its male users. My objective was to identify why male users don’t engage with the Pinterest app and understand any recurrent pain points.

STEP 1: Understanding the problem

Why is this problem important to solve?

  1. A win-win-win situation. When users feel happy about using it (i.e. find it helpful) -> businesses on the platform are benefitted because they can convert more ads to sales -> more profit given that revenue comes from ads
  2. 77% of users are women. There’s a skewed un-tapped market which means a large potential audience is being left out. Given that the main source of revenue for Pinterest are from ads, certain male brands won’t place ads on Pinterest because of the unbalanced gender ratio

Reframe Research Problem into Objectives

Objective 1: Understand how men look for ideas and inspirations, and how they keep their ideas organised and easy to find
Research goals: Identify why male users don’t engage with the Pinterest app

Objective 2: Identify the barriers that the users encounter when searching for images on Pinterest
Research goals: Identify recurrent or significant pain points

Key research method

In order to have a better understanding of the needs and pains behind male users, I conducted a qualitative interview with two male users and a brief competitive analysis

01 Qualitative interview

  • Tell me about the last time you used the search function in Pinterest.
  • What were you hoping to achieve?
  • What’s the reason why you pin certain content?
  • Name 3 adjectives you would associate Pinterest with. Can you tell me more about each choice of adjective?
  • What’s been your latest hobby or side project?
  • When you’re looking for inspirations and ideas for them, where would you go?
  • Why do you choose those platforms?

I separated the interview into 3 parts: Motivation for using Pinterest, Attitudes towards Pinterest, and User wants. I wanted to understand their initial reason for signing up and using Pinterest. I also wanted to explore their current perception towards the app. Lastly, I wanted to find out the alternatives to Pinterest and why men find those platforms more attractive.

Let’s summarise these insights into a problem statement, broken down for clarity:

"The concept of a mood board on its own does not appeal to men. When searching for inspirations and ideas online, men expect to find more content and information in addition to visual images. Hence, users who come to Pinterest needs to encounter more than visual information — otherwise, we will lose them to the next available source out there."
